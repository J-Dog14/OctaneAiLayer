## Plyo / Throwing — 3-day cycle · Starter · phase: Velocity Phase

> Simon Brutskiy, a starter in the Velocity Phase, exhibits key pitching deficits in pelvis obliquity, max horizontal abduction, and pelvis angular velocity, alongside lower Proteus shotput acceleration. This three-day plyo cycle is crafted to progressively address these inefficiencies while leveraging the Velocity Phase's allowance for higher intensity up to P2. The P0 and P1 sessions prioritize feel, connection, and gradual re-engagement of movement patterns following the Saturday game, with P0 focusing on recovery and P1 building light intent. The P2 session aggressively targets the hip drive and rotational deficits (pelvis obliquity and angular velocity) as well as horizontal abduction, aiming to translate power gains into throwing velocity. Due to the limited candidate drill pools provided for P1 and P2, these sessions utilize all available relevant drills, slightly under-meeting the specified drill count for those levels but maximizing impact with the given options. The weekly layout ensures a mandatory P0 post-game, only one P2 per week on game day, and a mid-week structural P0 for optimal recovery and preparation.
