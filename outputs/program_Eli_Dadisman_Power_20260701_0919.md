## Plyo / Throwing — 3-day cycle · Starter · phase: Velocity Phase

> Eli's dominant deficits revolve around trunk linear and angular velocity, indicating a need to improve hip-shoulder separation and rotational power. Additionally, lower body extension power (Proteus D2 extension) is a key area for development, impacting hip drive and lead leg mechanics. The 'Velocity Phase' annual phase allows for high-intensity work up to P3, so the P0, P1, P2 cycle can effectively target these inefficiencies. The P0 day focuses on feel and recovery, the P1 gradually introduces intent for movement pattern reinforcement, and the P2 day maximizes intent and specific corrections for hip drive, lead leg, and trunk rotation, preparing him for game day while adhering to the 'one P2 per week' rule.
