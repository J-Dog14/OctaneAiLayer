## Plyo / Throwing — 3-day cycle · Starter · phase: Velocity Phase

> Simon Brutskiy's plyo cycle is designed to address key deficits in pelvis rotation velocity and horizontal abduction (arm path), which contribute to his overall power output issues, as indicated by Proteus acceleration deficits. Given his 'Velocity Phase' and 'Starter' role with a Saturday game, the in-season weekly cadence dictates a P1 plyo session on game day (Saturday) as the game itself is the P2 event. This means his in-week plyo sessions emphasize recovery (P0) and rebuilding intent (P1), reserving the highest intensity for the actual game. The cycle balances high-intent work to improve pelvis and arm speed with recovery elements, ensuring durability while still pushing for velocity gains within the phase's guardrails. The limited pool of available drills per level, sourced from similar pitchers, restricts variety but allows for targeted repetition of foundational movements.
