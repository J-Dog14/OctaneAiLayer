## Plyo / Throwing — 3-day cycle · Starter · phase: Velocity Phase

> Eli Dadisman's plyo cycle prioritizes addressing his significant deficits in lead leg extension power (Proteus D2 extension, trunk linear velocity, PPU force) and improving thorax rotation speed, while also touching on horizontal abduction. The cycle leverages the 'Velocity Phase' to push intent and specific motor learning. P0 focuses on restoring feel and general movement, P1 introduces balanced intent, and P2 aggressively targets lead leg drive, hip-shoulder separation, and rotational speed to close Eli's primary kinematic gaps. The weekly layout provides a structured approach for a starter in an off-season velocity block, ensuring adequate recovery between high-intensity sessions.
