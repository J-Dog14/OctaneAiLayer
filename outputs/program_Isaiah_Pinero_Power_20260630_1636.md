## Plyo / Throwing — 3-day cycle · Starter · phase: Velocity Phase

> Isaiah Pinero's dominant deficits revolve around lead leg force absorption and blocking, coupled with a general lack of rotational power and velocity as highlighted by his pitching 3D and Proteus data. This three-day plyo cycle directly targets these inefficiencies across varying intensities. The P0 session focuses on gentle reconnection and feel, while P1 gradually introduces more specific rotational and lead leg work. The P2 session aggressively pushes for max intent, lead leg blocking, and rotational velocity, aligning with his 'Velocity Phase' annual training goal to develop arm speed and velocity ceiling. As a starter, his workload can tolerate the higher end of the prescribed volume, ensuring comprehensive development within the safety guardrails of the program.
