## Plyo / Throwing — 3-day cycle · Starter · phase: Velocity Phase

> Connor Hathaway's deficits in Proteus D2 extension velocity/acceleration and force plate jump metrics (AUC, velocity/force at peak power) indicate a clear need to improve hip drive, pelvis-trunk amplification, and overall lower-half intent. This three-day cycle progressively addresses these gaps, starting with recovery-focused feel work at P0, transitioning to moderate-intent dynamic drills at P1, and culminating in high-intensity drills at P2 that specifically target explosive hip rotation, lead leg drive, and ground force transfer. As Connor is a starter in the Velocity Phase, these levels are appropriate, allowing for maximum intent education and accumulation when prescribed, with sufficient recovery built into the cycle.
