## Plyo / Throwing — 3-day cycle · Starter · phase: Velocity Phase

> Eli's programming prioritizes addressing his deficits in lead leg drive and trunk rotational velocity, identified by his Proteus and 3D mechanics data, alongside improving horizontal abduction for a more efficient arm path. The P0 day focuses on low-intensity priming and tissue restoration, essential for recovery given his starter role and Saturday game day. P1 progressively builds moderate intent, incorporating drills that reinforce hip drive and arm path. P2 is designed for maximum intent expression, specifically selecting drills to aggressively target lead leg power and trunk rotation through dynamic movements and underload balls. This cycle supports his 'Velocity Phase' annual goal by enabling high-intent work while carefully managing recovery within an in-season context.
