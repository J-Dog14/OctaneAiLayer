## Bulletproofing

> Simon, as a power-focused high school pitcher, requires robust shoulder health programming. His 'max_horizontal_abduction' deficit indicates a need for increased posterior cuff strength and scapular stability to control the throwing motion and protect the anterior shoulder. This program prioritizes foundational pitcher staples alongside exercises specifically targeting posterior shoulder and scapular control to mitigate stress from high-velocity throwing.

| # | Slot | Exercise | Dose | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | cuff | Prone Shoulder Cuff Routine | 2 × 5 | Directly strengthens the posterior rotator cuff to improve shoulder deceleration and control during maximum horizontal abduction. |
| 2 | mid-low trap | Incline Ys | 2 × 10 | Activates the mid/lower traps and posterior deltoids to improve scapular stability and support the posterior shoulder. |
| 3 | scap stability | Blackburn Scap Series | 2 × 13 | Enhances overall scapular control and endurance, crucial for dynamic shoulder stability throughout the throwing motion. |
| 4 | cuff | Sleeper Stretch | 1 × 90s | Improves internal rotation mobility, a critical component of arm health for pitchers, addressing common deficits. |
| 5 | cuff | Wall Pec Stretch w/ ER | 1 × 30s | Stretches the anterior shoulder and pectorals while promoting external rotation, vital for overhead athletes. |
| 6 | cuff | Horizontal Abduction Scorpions | 1 × 90s | Enhances thoracic spine rotation and shoulder mobility, indirectly supporting shoulder health and throwing mechanics. |
| 7 | iso | Dead Hang Series | 1 × 90s | Provides shoulder joint decompression and traction, promoting glenohumeral health and overhead mobility. |

## Plyo / Throwing — 3-day cycle · Starter · phase: Velocity Phase

> This three-day plyo cycle for Simon Brutskiy targets his primary deficits in pelvis rotation/angular velocity and horizontal abduction, alongside improving explosive acceleration, which is a key feel goal. Given his 'Velocity Phase' annual calendar, the program emphasizes intent education and deficit correction, allowing for P2 work days while strictly adhering to P0 recovery days after high-intent throwing. The cycle balances high-intent work with crucial recovery, ensuring proper tissue restoration and pattern reinforcement. Due to the limited candidate drill pool for P1 and P2, the total drill count for these sessions is slightly lower than typical targets, prioritizing quality over volume from the available options.
