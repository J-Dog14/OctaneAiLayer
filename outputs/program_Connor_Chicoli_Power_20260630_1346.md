## Plyo / Throwing — 3-day cycle · Starter · phase: Velocity Phase

> This three-day plyo cycle for Connor Chicoli, a college starter in the Velocity Phase, primarily targets his deficits in total trunk flexion and lead leg force, as well as improving rotational power (Proteus D2 extension). The Velocity Phase allows for high-intensity work, so P2 days focus heavily on intent and addressing these mechanical gaps through aggressive rotational and lead-leg driven drills. P1 days offer a balanced approach, building on the feel for rotation and lead leg mechanics without over-spiking intent. P0 days are strictly for recovery and tissue restoration, maintaining basic movement patterns and feel, crucial for a starter in a high-intensity phase. The cycle is designed to progressively load these areas, maximizing power and efficiency throughout the week while ensuring adequate recovery.
