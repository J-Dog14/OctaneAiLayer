## Plyo / Throwing — 3-day cycle · Starter · phase: Velocity Phase

> Connor's dominant deficits include poor Proteus D2 extension velocity and acceleration, alongside lower body power issues (DJ and PPU metrics), suggesting struggles with rapidly accelerating through the rotational phase and efficient hip drive. The cycle addresses these by progressively emphasizing hip drive, kinematic sequence timing, and full-body intent, building from recovery to maximum effort. Given Connor is a starter in the Velocity Phase, all plyo levels (P0, P1, P2) are permitted, allowing for aggressive intent education and correction of these power and rotational inefficiencies without phase-based downgrades. The P0 day prioritizes tissue restoration and fundamental movement, P1 introduces moderate intent for pattern reinforcement, and P2 focuses on maximum intent expression to directly target the D2 extension and lower body power gaps.
