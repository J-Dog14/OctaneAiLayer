## Plyo / Throwing — 3-day cycle · Starter · phase: Velocity Phase

> Simon Martone's plyo cycle is designed to primarily address his lead knee at release and pelvis obliquity deficits, which manifest as issues in lead leg blocking and hip-shoulder separation. The Proteus deficits in power and velocity further reinforce the need for drills that emphasize rotational power and full-body intent. Since he is in the 'Velocity Phase', the P2 session pushes for maximum intent expression and direct inefficiency correction, while P0 and P1 gradually build foundational movement and recovery. The weekly layout prioritizes recovery immediately after his Saturday game, with P0 days, and incorporates P1 days to rebuild intent without overloading before his next start. Thursday is designated P0 for structural rest before the pre-game ramp, as per the in-season starter cadence.
