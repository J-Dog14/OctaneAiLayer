## Concrete recommendations (ranked by signal strength)

### 1. Move default plyo work day off Monday

Coaches assign P2 most often on **Saturday** (5 instances across these evals). Our skill defaults to Mon=P2, which is misaligned with the typical pitcher cadence (pen day mid-week). Fix in `skills/plyo-programming/SKILL.md` weekly layout guidance.

### 2. Pitcher BP staples are systematically missed

Top 5 missed BP exercises across evals: **Dead Hang Series** (8×), **Seated DB ER off Knee** (3×), **Young Stretch** (3×), **Blackburn Scap Series** (3×), **Pec Scorpion** (3×). These are arm-care staples coaches prescribe to nearly every pitcher regardless of Z-score deficit signal. Consider a *role-gated* (`has_pitching_data=True`) BP floor rather than a corpus-wide one.

### 3. Prep patterns systematically missed

Top movement patterns we never touch in prep: **med ball / rotational power** (12×), **saddle drill** (11×), **waterbag / unpredictable load** (10×). Worth checking whether the prep candidate pool contains any exercises matching these patterns at all, or whether the skill prompt needs explicit slot guidance for them.

### 4. Lift patterns we over-prescribe vs coaches

Top patterns we add that coaches don't include: **med ball / rotational power** (39×), **plyometric coordination** (23×), **hip-hinge / posterior chain** (20×). If sprint or jump patterns dominate this list, our default of outputting all 5 lift templates (Legs/Upper/Total Body/Sprint/Jump) is too broad. Coaches at the position-player level often skip Sprint or Jump days entirely.
