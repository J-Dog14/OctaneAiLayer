## Plyo / Throwing — 3-day cycle · Starter · phase: Velocity Phase

> Barron's primary mechanical deficit is low pelvis angular velocity at foot contact, indicating a need for improved hip drive and rotational power. His Proteus deficits, despite positive Z-scores, highlight areas where D2 extension acceleration and power could be optimized, aligning with higher intent and kinetic chain efficiency. The Velocity Phase allows for high-intensity work up to P3, but without an explicit P3 directive, we cap at P2. This cycle targets hip drive and intent expression progressively, with P0 prioritizing recovery and foundational connection, P1 building feel and moderate intent, and P2 pushing max intent and direct correction of pelvis rotation and overall kinetic chain acceleration. The drills were selected to directly address these deficits and reinforce proper arm path.
