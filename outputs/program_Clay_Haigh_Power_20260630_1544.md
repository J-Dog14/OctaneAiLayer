## Prep block

> Clay Haigh's prep focuses on improving significant thoracic and shoulder mobility deficits, activating core and single-leg stability, and priming his power system. The plan includes a general warmup, targeted mobility for the T-spine and shoulders, activation for core and glutes (addressing SLV asymmetry), and CNS priming before concluding with three movement enhancement drills suited for a power athlete. Due to the limitations of the candidate pool, direct work for cervical lateral flexion and radial nerve glide deficits is not included.

| # | Slot | Exercise | Dose | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | general warmup | Pull Back Butt Kick to Walking Spiderman w/ Hip Lift and Reach | 1 × 5 | A dynamic full-body movement to increase core temperature and prepare major joints for work. |
| 2 | mobility | Wall Slides w/ liftoff | 1 × 8 | Improves shoulder flexion, scapular control, and thoracic extension, directly targeting the back-to-wall shoulder flexion deficit. |
| 3 | mobility | Bretzel 2.0 | 1 × 6 | Offers comprehensive hip and T-spine rotation mobility, crucial for rotational power athletes. |
| 4 | activation | Pelvic Tilt Series | 1 × 7 | Activates deep core muscles and improves lumbopelvic control, essential for stability. |
| 5 | activation | TRX Serratus Slides | 1 × 8 | Activates the serratus anterior for improved scapular stability and shoulder health. |
| 6 | activation | Single Leg Hip Thrust Off Wall | 1 × 6 | Targets glute activation and single-leg stability to address the right single-leg vertical jump asymmetry. |
| 7 | cns prep | Low Intensity Pogos | 2 × 8 | Primes the nervous system for elastic power, addressing deficits in plyometric performance like DJ RSI. |
| 8 | movement enhancement | Waterbag Skenes | 1 × 6 | Introduces rotational power and trunk stability, bridging into the session's power demands. |

## Bulletproofing

> This bulletproofing program prioritizes improving shoulder flexion and scapular control, directly addressing the significant 'back to wall shoulder flexion' deficit. It combines rotator cuff strength, scapular stabilization, and targeted mobility work crucial for a throwing athlete in a power phase.

| # | Slot | Exercise | Dose | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | cuff | Seated DB ER off Knee | 2 × 10 | Strengthens the rotator cuff, essential for external rotation strength and deceleration in throwing athletes. |
| 2 | mid-low trap | Incline Ys | 2 × 12 | Activates the mid and lower traps, crucial for scapular upward rotation and posterior tilt during overhead movements. |
| 3 | scap stability | Blackburn Scap Series | 2 × 12 | Enhances overall scapular control and stability, supporting healthy shoulder mechanics and injury prevention. |
| 4 | iso | Dead Hang Series | 2 × 60s | Provides shoulder decompression and improves passive overhead mobility, aiding the 'back to wall shoulder flexion' deficit. |
| 5 | iso | Wall Pec Stretch w/ ER | 2 × 30s | Addresses anterior shoulder and chest tightness, which often restricts full shoulder flexion and range of motion. |
