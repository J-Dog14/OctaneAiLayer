## Plyo / Throwing — 3-day cycle · Starter · phase: Velocity Phase

> Ryan Chasse's primary mechanical deficits are in hip-shoulder separation and trunk rotation relative to the pelvis at foot contact, indicating a need to improve rotational power transfer and kinematic sequencing. His arm external rotation timing also shows room for improvement. The feel goal across this cycle is to enhance hip drive and overall intent/effort, which will directly address these power and timing deficiencies. As Ryan is in the Velocity Phase, all plyo levels (P0, P1, P2) are permitted without downgrades, allowing for a structured build-up from recovery-oriented feel work to high-intent, deficit-targeted training. The P0 days focus on tissue restoration and connection, P1 introduces balanced intent and mild correction, while P2 aggressively targets his rotational power gaps with max effort drills to drive velocity and refine sequencing.
