## Plyo / Throwing — 3-day cycle · Starter · phase: Velocity Phase

> Connor's deficit profile highlights significant issues with Proteus D2 extension velocity and acceleration, indicating a need to improve hip-shoulder separation and rotational power through the latter phases of his delivery. His force plate data further supports a general deficit in lower-half power and ground force application. The P0 and P1 sessions prioritize feel, connection, and foundational hip rotation (e.g., Reverse Throws, OG Pivots, Hip Rotation Pivots) to build the neuromuscular pathways for better energy transfer. The P2 session aggressively targets these deficits with drills like Mound Step Behinds and HAP Throws to maximize intent and power expression, aligning perfectly with his 'Velocity Phase' annual goal. The weekly layout strictly adheres to post-game and post-pen P0 days, ensuring adequate recovery for a starter with Saturday games.
