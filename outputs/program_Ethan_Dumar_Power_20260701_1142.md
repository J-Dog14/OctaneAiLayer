## Plyo / Throwing — 3-day cycle · Starter · phase: Velocity Phase

> Ethan Dumar's primary deficit is a significant lack of lead leg force production (vertical and resultant peak force), which impacts his ability to efficiently transfer energy. The plyo cycle is designed to progressively address this by emphasizing hip drive, lead leg bracing, and dynamic force application. As Ethan is in the 'Velocity Phase,' the program can aggressively target inefficiency correction and intent expression, even incorporating P2 work mid-week. The cycle progresses from tissue recovery and foundational hip connection at P0, through balanced feel and moderate lead-leg focus at P1, to max-intent lead-leg drive and force transfer drills at P2. This structure allows for targeted development while respecting recovery needs within his off-season training phase.
