## Plyo / Throwing — 3-day cycle · Starter · phase: Velocity Phase

> Eli's primary deficits revolve around a lack of rotational power/velocity in the trunk (evidenced by low max trunk linear/angular velocity) and restricted horizontal abduction. His Proteus D2 extension power also highlights a need for better hip-shoulder separation and arm whip. The feel goal for this cycle is to enhance rotational sequencing, improve hip-shoulder separation, and optimize his arm path. Being in the Velocity Phase, all P0, P1, and P2 levels are permitted, allowing for aggressive intent work on P2 days while respecting recovery. The cycle progressively increases intensity, with P0 focused on feel and recovery, P1 on balanced intent, and P2 on maximizing rotational power and correcting specific mechanical inefficiencies.
