# Plyo Day Cadence — Starter vs. Reliever

The plyo level for any given day is a function of where the athlete is in their weekly cadence. The cadence differs significantly between starters and relievers because their recovery and outing patterns differ.

## Starter Cadence (5-day rotation)

A starter pitches on a fixed schedule, with recovery and ramp-up days between outings. The standard 5-day cadence:

| Day | Plyo Level | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Day 0 — Start day | **P2** | The outing itself is the high-intent throw of the week. Plyos before the start are P2 to prime intent and movement quality. |
| Day +1 — Day after start | **P0** | Non-negotiable recovery. Connective tissue is recovering whether the athlete feels good or not. |
| Day +2 — Recovery → return | **P1** | Hybrid. Restore feel without spiking intent. |
| Day +3 — 3 days out | **P2** | Highest-intent plyo day of the recovery cycle. Pen day for many starters. |
| Days +4 / +5 — Late cycle | **Varies** | Assess fatigue markers, day-to-day feel, and days of rest remaining before next start. Lean P1 if a 5-day cycle, lean P2 → recovery if a 6-day cycle. |

## Reliever Cadence (variable)

Relievers don't pitch on a fixed schedule. Their plyo programming is more reactive to actual appearances. Rules:

- **Day after any appearance** → P0. Always. Whether they threw 5 pitches or 25 pitches.
- **2+ days since last appearance** → P1 or P2 depending on athlete feel and coach read. If they look ready, P2. If they're stiff or tired, P1.
- **Back-to-back appearances** — if the reliever threw on consecutive days, treat the day after the *second* appearance as P0 even if 48 hours have passed since the first appearance. Two appearances accumulates more workload than the calendar suggests.
- **Long stretches without appearances** — when a reliever hasn't pitched in 4+ days, the cadence resets. Treat them like a starter in a midweek slot until they appear again. P2 work is acceptable here to maintain readiness.

## How role affects level choice

In addition to day-of-week, role matters because starters and relievers handle volume differently:

- **Starters tolerate higher per-session plyo volume** — they have predictable recovery windows. Sessions can hit the upper end of the level's exercise count and rep ranges.
- **Relievers tolerate less per-session plyo volume** — uncertain workload means we protect them by staying at the lower end of the level's exercise count and rep ranges.

When you have the level but you're unsure about the upper bound (e.g., "5–7 exercises" for P2), pick the *lower* number for relievers and the *upper* number for starters.

## In-season exceptions

When in-season:

- Reduce overall volume across all levels — favor the lower end of every range.
- P3 is almost never assigned during the season. Save intent education for the off-season.
- Even on P2 days, prioritize feel + intent over volume accumulation. The competitive load is already there; we just complement it.

## Off-season starting points

When the athlete is in the off-season (Recovery, Rebuild Capacity, Workload Ramp, or Velocity phases per the annual system):

- Cadence rules above still apply but reset weekly because there are no real outings.
- A typical off-season week might look like: P2 — P0 — P1 — P2 — P0 — light or off — light catch.
- Off-season is the right time to push P3 if the athlete has earned it.

## Quick-check decision tree

1. Did the athlete throw yesterday (real outing OR pen, not light catch)?
   - **Yes** → P0, no exceptions.
2. Is the athlete in the annual In-Season phase AND does their schedule have them pitching tomorrow?
   - **Yes** → P1 (priming) or P2 (depending on intent). Skip if recovery suggests it.
3. Is the athlete 2-3 days out from a planned high-intent throw or outing?
   - **Yes** → P2.
4. Otherwise → P1 by default.
5. P3 only if the coach has explicit reason ("athlete doesn't understand intent yet").
6. Cross-check against `references/annual-throwing-system.md` — if the phase prohibits the level you'd pick, downgrade to the highest allowed.
